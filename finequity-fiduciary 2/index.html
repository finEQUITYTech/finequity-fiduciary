Board Oversight Reference Bot

Paste a transaction from your credit card statement — vendor name, amount, and date — and ask what it is.

The bot can identify known vendors, check grant or budget alignment, flag possible concerns, and suggest one precise question to ask staff.

🔒 This app does not store conversation history in its own database. Messages are sent to Anthropic through finEQUITY’s Netlify function to generate a response.

How to get started

Open the Divvy or Brex statement from Briane alongside this bot.

Scan the statement. Trust your gut. Pick 1–3 items that stand out:

  • A vendor name you do not recognize
  • An amount that seems unusually large
  • Something that looks like cash, Venmo, Zelle, or gift cards
  • A description that does not make clear what was purchased

This is a spot-check, not a formal audit.

What this bot can reference

Vendor knowledge baseKnown QBO vendors from Jan–May 2026.
Grant and budget tagging guideJPMC, Citi Foundation, Emerson/SVCF, and Camelback rules.
Fraud pattern remindersGift cards, duplicates, cash-like payments, and unclear vendors.
ReceiptsNot available here. Ask staff for specific receipts.
Individual salariesNot accessible and not in scope.

Fraud red flags

  • Gift cards: Amazon, Apple, Target, Walmart at gift-card-like amounts
  • Cash-like payments: Venmo, Zelle, Square Cash, wires to personal accounts
  • Duplicates: same vendor and amount within days
  • Personal spend: grocery stores or gas stations on a company card
  • Blank vendor: no usable vendor description
